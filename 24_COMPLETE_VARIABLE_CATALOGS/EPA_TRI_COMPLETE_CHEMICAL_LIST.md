## üîç HOW TO USE THIS CATALOG FOR RESEARCH

### **Step 1: Determine Chemicals of Interest**

**By Health Concern:**
1. Search official PDF for "carcinogen" or "OSHA"
2. Filter GuideME database by health effect category
3. Focus on PBT chemicals for bioaccumulation studies

**By Industry:**
1. Manufacturing ‚Üí metals, solvents, plastics
2. Agriculture ‚Üí pesticides, ammonia, nitrates
3. Energy ‚Üí mercury, arsenic, sulfur dioxide
4. Chemical production ‚Üí acids, bases, intermediates

### **Step 2: Extract County-Level Data**

**TRI National Analysis:** https://www.epa.gov/trinationalanalysis
- Pre-calculated national and state summaries
- Trend analysis 1988-present
- Top chemicals and industries

**TRI Basic Plus Files:** https://www.epa.gov/toxics-release-inventory-tri-program/tri-basic-plus-data-files-guides
- Facility-level data with county FIPS codes
- All chemicals reported
- All release pathways (air, water, land, off-site)

**TRI Data Download Tool:** https://www.epa.gov/toxics-release-inventory-tri-program/tri-data-and-tools
- Custom queries by chemical, county, year
- Export to CSV/Excel

### **Step 3: Link to Health Outcomes**

**County-level analysis:**
```r
library(tidyverse)

# Download TRI data with county FIPS
tri_data <- read_csv("TRI_basic_plus_data.csv")

# Aggregate to county-year-chemical
county_chemical_releases <- tri_data %>%
  group_by(FIPS_CODE, REPORTING_YEAR, CHEMICAL) %>%
  summarize(
    total_releases_lbs = sum(TOTAL_RELEASES, na.rm = TRUE),
    air_releases_lbs = sum(FUGITIVE_AIR + STACK_AIR, na.rm = TRUE),
    water_releases_lbs = sum(SURFACE_WATER_DISCHARGES, na.rm = TRUE),
    facilities_count = n_distinct(FACILITY_ID)
  )

# Focus on carcinogens
carcinogens <- c("Benzene", "Arsenic", "Cadmium", "Chromium",
                 "Nickel", "Vinyl chloride", "Formaldehyde")

county_carcinogen_exposure <- county_chemical_releases %>%
  filter(CHEMICAL %in% carcinogens)
```

### **Data Quality & Limitations**

- TRI covers only facilities meeting reporting thresholds
- Not all releases are monitored (some are estimated)
- Excludes mobile sources, small businesses, agriculture
- Underreports actual environmental chemical burden

### **Companion Data Sources**

- **EPA Air Quality System (AQS):** Ambient air monitoring (complements TRI)
- **EPA Enforcement & Compliance History Online (ECHO):** Facility violations
- **ATSDR ToxProfiles:** Toxicological profiles for priority chemicals
